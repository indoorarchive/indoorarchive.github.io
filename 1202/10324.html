If you haven't heard, there's more good news from Kent. Additional time will be available for flying on Saturday, 4/14, from 4 pm to 10 pm. This will be held as a record trial. The cost will be $10 per person per hour. This news is especially good for out of town fliers, who now can enjoy this world class site for two days.

Kent is where most of the AMA Cat II records have been set; more are sure fall this year with the two days.

There are a number of interesting challenges and "matches" this year. I will get the pot stirring ...

The A6 battle continues at Kent. Bill Gowen, current Cat I, II, and IV A6 record holder and 2007 USIC champion, faces a daunting group of A6 challengers. Multi-time USIC A6 champion, Tom Sova, will defend his local turf against the outsiders. Tom tells me that he has built a special A6 just for Kent. I can't wait to see it. Then there are the Kansas City A6 specialists: Gary Hodson, Tem Johnson, and Emil Schutzel. Each of these gentlemen have a USIC A6 belt under him. Gary narrowly lost to Bill at Tustin in Decmeber and is sure to be keen for a second battle. These five gents -- Bill, Tom, Gary, Tem, and Emil -- together represent all of the USIC A6 winners. Don't miss this rare opportunity to witness these gents fly against each other.

John Kagan will be looking to get back his Cat II F1D record. In his path will be the current Cat II F1D record holder, Mark Bennett, and the current Cat IV F1D record holder Brett Sanborn.

Speaking of Kagan, does everyone remember that he broke the Cat II SCLG record last year? He took that record from none other than Mr. Jim Buxton. I hope Jim is ready to re-take his record; if not, he will have to start flying F1D to regain his honor.

Another record that is on the line is F1L. The current record is held by Larry Coslick at 16:06. Last year, this writer broke this mark with a late flight of 16:23. However, there was a "small" problem: the model weighed 1.17g instead of the minimum 1.20g. Thoroughly embarrassed and disappointed, he vowed to return this year and break the mark again with a 1.20000g model. Will the extra 30 mg hold him back? We will see.

More observations later...

Enjoy,

-Kang
