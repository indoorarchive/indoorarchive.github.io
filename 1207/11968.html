Returning to an old subject.

While visiting my local hardware store, I bumped into a small soldering
iron made by (German company) Stannol. 12 volts, 7.5 watts, the iron is
shorter than a pencil, and some 2 to 3 mm in diameter. Seemed to work
nice for covering some new F1D models. It has a lead, which is a slight
shortcoming, but the light lead is not as heavy as for 220 (or 110) volt
irons. Actually, with current less than one ampere, one could install a
lithium battery for a handle and make it portable...

Could not find it on stannol web page, but found it at amazon:
http://www.amazon.co.uk/Stannol-Soldering-Needle-12-V/dp/B002BZFQ1A
(also at Conrad in Germany).
