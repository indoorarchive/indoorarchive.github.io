A few results deserve extra mention. Moscow, Idaho is situated at an altitude of 2600 ft. The altitude results in serious performance loss compared to sea level. As an example, my EZB flies with an average RPM of 64 at Johnson City but turns a fast 69 RPM at the Kibbie.

John Sayre's HLG 2 flight total of 180s (90,90). I believe these are site records. I saw a couple of John's practice flights, and his launches were very high, about 120 ft. In 2008, the big guns of HLG were at the Kibbie for the "Battle Near Seattle", and the winning time was 158 by Stan Buddenbohm.

Emil Schutzel's 13:11 Ministick flight.

Royce Chung, who is a junior flier, winning Limited Pennyplane with a time of 13:42.

Larry Coslick's 29:12 EZB flight. There has only been a handful of 29+ minute flights at the Kibbie.

Jake Palmer's 1/2 A winning time 12:37. This is a site record.

Tim Chang's 35:02 in Intermediate Stick. Tim is an incredibly good flier. I believe he holds the Kibbie site record in F1D. Look out for him in the F1D Team Selection contest in 2013.

Larry Coslick's 34:55 in Intermediate Stick.
