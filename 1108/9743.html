I took a pic of a Treger hub that Geoffrey (from UK) bought, it seems to
be also 3 turns spring.

Talking of which (the springs), I discussed 'em with the Brits, and
learned an interesting concept about "hard" and "soft" springs. Looks
like the one that I have been using, with 7 turns, is in the "soft
category", resulting in an abtrupt change of pitch when it eventually
happens. And with a harder spring I would have the prop staying longer
"on the spring", making the change in the altitude while on the spring
more variable. I guess the next step is to measure the motor torque
curve while unwinding, measure the VP action, and try to figure the
torque -> pitch & RPM -> output power pattern of the flight, then think
if a different pattern of changes would be more efficient.

I have a feeling that I have just opened a new door slightly, and have
taken a brief peek into the room.... Wonder what I'll find there :-)
