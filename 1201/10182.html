There hasn't been any precedent that I know of.

Usually, body heat columns have much more of a negative impact than positive - a big stall and loss of altitude.

However, Treger's F1D had the best reaction to strong lift that I've seen. It rose without much change in pitch and continued happily along, even at the low power end of the flight. I suspect it has a forward CG, close to the center of pressure. Guess what I'm going to be testing for the next WC :)
