> I have seen Kevlar used for motorstick bracing but can't remember who
> has used it. I'd like to try it on a 35 cm motorstick. How many
> strands would be sufficient? Is it difficult to glue or does it have
> to be tied off on the thrust bearing and rear hook? Any
> suggestions? Tom Sova
>
> [Non-text portions of this message have been removed]

Tom,

I have used Kevlar on Int Stick and F1D (old rules). I believe I used
4-5 strands on my Int Stick (models in the 700mg or less range). I have
2 strands on my 35 cm model. I treat it like wire, I always wrap around
front bearing and rear hook then glue it to the bracing posts. I think
it holds much better than wire. The wire seems to always slip. That is
why I switched to Kevlar wing ans stab bracing, even on my HLS model.

Don
