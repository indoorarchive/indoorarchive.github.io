> I think that model designs go in cycles with various things being
> tried. EZB design has changed dramatically since Ron Williams wrote
> his book. It is common to have a model where the CG is near or
behind
> the trailing edge of the wing.
>
> The best thing to do would be to do a bit of experimentation and to
> consider more than just the CG location. Look at the size of the
stab
> and the length of the tailboom as well as the incidence angles. They
> all work together. To find the best combination, keep good notes and
> use your stopwatch. Small changes can have a big impact on
performance.

> Make your next model a more modern design, try one of the plans out
