The Alexei carbon tube, 6mm diameter with a 0.1mm wall thickness, weighs just under 90mg per inch.  For comparison, a .250" diameter (about 6mm) balsa tube made from 0.013" thick sheet of 4# density (about 0.3mm) weighs about 11mg per inch.  This is a typical tube used for F1D motor sticks.

 

Such observations are a constant reminder to me of what an amazing material balsa is.

 

Leo

re: carbon tubes. There is an interesting thread on RCGRoups: http://www.rcgroups.com/forums/showthread.php?t=1158259&page=4

Post no. 54 is the beginning of a discussion on modeler made carbon tubes. I read all of it some time ago and don't remember how complete the discussion was but the tubes for these planes were reputed to be very strong and light, much more so that any manufactured carbon fiber tubing. 

The plane described has had a large influence on the design of such indoor aerobatic RC planes and the development has been steady and thorough over the last few years. The difference in the flight of the planes from 2010 until now as shown on the various videos is really noticeable.

Alexei seems to be quite willing to be helpful and might have information that could be useful for indoor FF construction.
