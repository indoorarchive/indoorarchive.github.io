 This is very sad news.   I met Bob in 1997 at the College of DuPage, in Wheaton, Il. where there was a group of indoor modelers flying in a small cafeteria area, it had about 30ft ceiling on Monday nights.  I told him I was interested in gliders and he quickly responded if I show up next week,  he would teach me how to build a good indoor glider.  The next week he came with plans, wood, sanding blocks and  glue.  He showed me how to sand a wing and build a good indoor glider,  many of the techniques he showed me I still use today.  I learned a lot from Bob and enjoyed watching his models fly (they always flew great).  So I too,  am glad to have known him.   
Kurt Krempetz

  I know this news has been passed around locally, but not to the listserv.
Unfortunately I found out the day after the funeral memorial had taken place.
http://www.legacy.com/obituaries/chicagotribune/obituary.aspx?n=Robert-Warmann&pid=182916219

One of our great local competitors, Bob was quite the character.
The last time I saw him was at an outdoor contest at Bong in 2015.
He was flying a catapult launch glider. He had it trimmed ok and was wanting to put up an official flight.
This was a typical summer day at Bong, hot and thermally.
We knew a thermal was coming just from the air.
Bob just waited and waited and waited.
Someone calls out to him that he was going to miss the thermal.
"Bah!' he called out dismissively, looking around, still checking the air.

FINALLY, he pointed the glider upwards and let her rip - and it transitioned right into the center of a boomer.
Next thing we know it is a speck in the air. I was the only one who could still see it.
It maxed out and Bob thought it was lost forever. I never took my eyes off of it, knowing I would lose it if I did.

Luckily it wasn't windy, so it didn't drift more than 1/4 mile away. It popped out of the thermal and I got a good bead on it as it came down and found it within a couple of minutes of arriving in the general area.
Bob was waiting in his car along the road and gave me a ride back to the flying area, very gracious, very thankful that it was recovered.

I am glad to have known him.

Regards.
