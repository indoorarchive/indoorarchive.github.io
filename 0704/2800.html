 The air was good for most of Saturday where you launched it went up coming down in same place. F1L was a heated contest to very end with Chuck Marcos, Larry Loucka, Tom Sova (I think) and Doug Schaefer flying single 16 plus minute flights. Doug won by four seconds Larry and then Tom in third. Larry's 16 minute flight came late in evening after air had become ruff. Jim Richond had very good F1L flight going till hanging up near eight minutes. There were about six flying F1D regional Nick Ray made skillful adjustments to his model putting good times up. Doug's Intermediate Stick was at 50 foot coming down when motor broke landing with 27:54 which was exact same time of Tom Sova's but Tom won because of two seconds steering deduction of Doug's, seemed such odd time to break a motor. Several flew Mini stick with Larry L. winning with 11 minute plus with Tom second. A new person from St. Louis who came up with Larry C. won Limited Penny Plane with Jim Richmond second. Kurt K. and his dad plus few other did glid
ers in morning. Kurt had a neat towed line glider first I have ever seen. Our Junior World Champion Justin Young worked on VP both days.

 The heating and A/C weren't running above offices this year.
