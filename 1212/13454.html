I'm just following the lead of our last two world champions, Ivan and Lutz. They use boron on their stabs.

Earlier in my indoor flying (many, many moons ago), I was told that my EZB tail boom was too stiff -- a more flexible boom was needed for the launch. It just didn't jive with my way of thinking. Today, I prefer having a stiff boom -- relatively speaking for the light EZB models I fly.

Recently, I held a couple of Larry Coslick's EZB and F1L models. Both had very flexible tail booms. Clearly, it works for Larry. If your model flies as well as Larry's, then whatever you're doing is correct.
