That's probably a better system than what I
used if you want to restore the strength of the pole to something close to the
original. I have found that gluing the splits together with thin CA works for a
temporary repair.
 
For my poles I glued the splits, sanded them
down smooth and then used 1/2 oz fiberglass applied with CA to do a permanent
repair. I spread the fiberglass out on my work surface, dusted the broken
area of the pole with 3M77, rolled the pole in the glass, used a soft brush to
brush the fiberglass smooth, soaked the whole glassed area with thin CA and used
tissues to wipe off the excess. I cleaned the excess 3M77 off with lighter
fluid. It seems to have worked pretty well. I had to repair three damaged areas
that were probably close to a foot long each.
