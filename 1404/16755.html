Here are my opinions:
(2) Lutz Schramm's wing and stab is covered with OS film but the prop and
rudder are Y2K2
Lutz's model with the collapsed motor stick was covered with Y2K2
(17) Uwe Bundesen's model is covered with Y2K2, but his prop is OS
(1) Ivan Treger's model is covered with Y2K2
(4) Tony Hebb's model is covered with OS film
(5) Denzo Orsovai's model is covered with Y2K2
(15) Andres Ree's model is covered with Y2K2
(11) Aurel Popa's model appears to have a wing and prop coved with OS film
and stab covered with Y2K
(16)Gabriela Kaplanova's model is covered with OS film
(9)Klara Kaplanova's model is covered with OS film
(7)Mikita Kaplan's model is covered with OS film (though its possible he's
holding another team member's model)

Those are the only ones I can tell confidently from the pictures. I have
included rankings of each flyer from the 2013 Eurochamps. Since, I figured
that's where this data would lead.

Tony Hebb and Lutz Schramm both flew OS covered models. I think that Y2K2
is more a sign of how long someone has been in the hobby rather than
precondition for success if 1.2g 55cm. It is worth noting that Brett
Sanborn and John Kagan finished 3rd and 4th at the 2012 Word Champs using
OS film.

Regards,

Nick
