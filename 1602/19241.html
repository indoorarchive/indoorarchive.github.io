I'd be interested in a Geauga stripper replica if the price could be brought down somewhat. Even if not I'd still be interested, but would have to wait a bit to budget it out.
 

 I also second Tapio's remarks about a 3-d printed winder. You wouldn't be able to make it smaller, but it would be a definite boon to the hobby, especially if there were built-in fittings for a turns counter. Price would be considerably lower than a traditional winder, and if it got damaged, replacement would be cheaper as well. It might nevertheless require SLA printing, which does amp up the price a bit, but the quality would be excellent too.
