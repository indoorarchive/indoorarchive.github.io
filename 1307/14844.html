Gary
As far as my model is concerned the only problem I know of is that my
covering has gotten very wrinkled and baggy. Other than that there was
an rpm increase that seemed to push me over the edge of what length
motor I could use. Normally I can run motors up to 21" without too many
problems but at Kibbie anything over 19 was causing problems with rubber
bunching at the rear hook. I tried a number of solutions that seemed to
help but my times never got to what I expected.

I normally depend on turns left in the motor to keep the motor fairly
tight toward the end of the flight but the increased rpm's were
defeating that plan. My first test flight was the best I did in 2 days.
It was 8:44 and dead sticked at about 30' up. That would have won the
contest but I never reached that time again on my official flights.
