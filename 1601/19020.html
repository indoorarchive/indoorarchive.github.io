> If anyone is interested in learning more about microfilm, you should look at the November 1963 INAV. It includes an article about measuring thickness that was developed by Bill Bigge. It includes a chart relating the color to the thickness. Bill also weighed some MicroDyne microfilm and you can use that weight along with the chart to estimate how much a given color of film should weigh. His weight puts red violet film, which is slightly thicker than straw brown, at approximately 17mg/100si. Silver film should weigh 10-12mg/100si, or about a third the weight of the lightest Y2K2.
