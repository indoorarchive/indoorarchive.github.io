I'm irked by Don D.'s comment. Calling the Kibbie Dome a non-contest
insults the people who have organized it, attended it, and those who seek
to perpetuate it. I would think as someone who has organized a recent NATs
(all of which have had low attendance), he would have more respect for the
work that goes into organizing a contest of similar magnitude.

The Kibbie Dome is the only open access Cat IV site left in the United
States, and for this reason we need to preserve it.

 I've made the trip out to the Kibbie Dome the last three years. If you
plan ahead, it is doable for a total cost of $1000, even coming from the
East Coast. It really just comes down to priorities, and I think we are at
a point where we can't say, "I'll go to that contest next year" because
everyone else will keep it going in the meantime.

Regards,

Nick Ray
