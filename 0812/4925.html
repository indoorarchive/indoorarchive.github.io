--- In Indoor_Construction_at_yahoogroups.com, <RLBailey@...> wrote:
>
> When adjusting the stick bracing on an F1D fuselage, there are 2
principal points at which the adjustment can be made, ie at the front
wing post and at the centre post. Using one instead of the other could
affect the downthrust change vs elevator change.
>
> Any thoughts?

I assume your bracing is supported at two points? (mine just has one
post at the stick midpoint).

Even so, I'd be surprised if there was much difference between
adjusting it at one point vs. the other. I think the post's resistance
to bending would be much less than the force of either the motor stick
trying to straighten out or the motor trying to bend it.
