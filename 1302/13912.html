Alan was telling me how you work with your EZB’s and I was telling him that
for now, I would be afraid to handle planes as light as yours.

 

I built the Geodetic wing EZB from Ron’s book and made it with the larger
propeller (14” X 21) but, the prop block was all wrong to the point that I
was getting negative pitch. It wouldn’t fly. Alan and Art suggested that I
cut about ¼” of the end of the prop off and I did so and it flew real well.
Crazy as it may seem, I didn’t time the flight so, next month I know I’ll be
tweaking and adjusting to get best flight time from this EZB then, onto the
next lighter one.

 

Be well and take care,
