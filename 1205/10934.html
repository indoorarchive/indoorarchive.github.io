Ren and Steve, hold off two or three days until Shadow posts his build thread on HPA. I sent him all the parts that I use, including 3mm Lexan that I get from Aircraft Spruce instead of the .093 that Home Depot has. I also use a nice toggle switch from Mouser. It's a better unit than the Radio Shack switches that I originally used and the price is competitive because I buy 10 at a time. The only difference is that the kit has a 1/16 hook shaft, and the ones I make have 1/32, which is harder to fabricate.

Between the material that I e-mail, and Shadow's presentation, you should be able to make a decision on whether you want to (A) buy a kit ($75), (B) buy a ready to go winder ($130), or (C) source your own parts and material.
