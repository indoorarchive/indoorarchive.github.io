Since the European Championships were held last week, It has been suggested that I give another try at rounding up Kibbie 2015 attendees. So, if anyone plans to attend Kibbie 2015, please let me know (private message), fill out the poll on the question, or do whatever else to can think off. Obviously, if you have already dona any of the above, your plans have been noted in our current possible attendee list.

 Kibbie is a well lit, 150' high site. There are ample flying areas for different class planes. Helium will be available. The hours of flying start at 6:30am and end at 10pm for the first four days and end around 5pm on the last day. Gliders will be given priority parts of the day, perhaps in the beginning and the end of the day. Tables and chairs will be available so all one needs to bring are the planes and related equipment. We usually get a good rate at the local Best Western University Inn (a nice place to stay with great breakfasts included and with a convenient bar with one freed drink per day for registered guests) and there are even less expensive places to stay, too. Flying into Spokane International Airport makes for a 90 minute drive to the site in Moscow, Idaho. The weather is usually very pleasant in late June and early July.
