My name is Christopher Singleton (aka CaptainBalsa) and a year ago, I
returned to aeromodeling after an absence of about 12-14 years.
 
As in the past, I have mixed interests that include R/C, F/F and C/L. As
well, I have lots of things on the building board and although I love
building . . . I still hate covering.
 
What pulled me back into the hobby last year, is that I am a Scout Leader
and my co-leader (a private pilot) and I decided to use Aviation as our
troop specialty. This led to a series of projects for the troop that
included: a HLG (200% Pipsqueak glider), rubber power (Flying Squirrel),
sort-of-a-semi-scale-like-not-so-good-glider (Wright Glider by F. Scott),
CLG that-flew-too-good-for-our-small-gym (Phantom F4) and some model
rockets. Additionally the Scouts visited a fantastic R/C fun fly and two
aviation museums. Unfortunately, we ran out of time and never completed the
C/L sections . . . but a good time was had by all.
 
Over the past year I have built a variety of models ranging from Bill Hannon
and Walt Mooney as well as three C/L and two R/C models.
 
Currently the building board is crowded with a Hanger Rat, a Guillow's Bird
Dog, a Corben Super-Ace (40"ws, electric R/C) and a Dumas Pilatus Porter
(39" ws, electric R/C).
 
Although not yet an FAC member, I have on occasion flown indoor F/F with the
Tigertown FAC Sqdn #40 in Burlington, Ontario, Canada.
 
Best Regards,
Christopher Singleton
a.k.a. CaptainBalsa
