  Bruce your statement is the core problem with checking models on commerical
  airlines.

  After doing this for many years I am convinvced that the box must NOT look
  like a steamer trunk or suitcase, because if it does it will be treated as
  such. My boxes are all homeamde, out of 1/8" ply or 1/4" ply. They are
  neatly varnished and trimmed with hardware and say DELICATE INSTRUMENTS and
  TOP LOAD PLEASE. I've talked to a lot of gate agents and they rarely put my
  boxes on the standard conveyors because they are home made and LOOK fragile.
  Instead my boxes usually get hand carried to the ramp truck which drives
  them to the airplane.

  I think the words DELICATE INSTRUMENTS are crucial. They make the baggage
  handlers think "Geez, this must be a $10,000 telescope or something. I am
  SCREWED if I damage it!" In contrast the words MODEL AIRPLANES seems trivial
  to the average baggage handler.
