Kang,

I'm not saying your doing it consciously. If EZB was included in the
proposed events I would say this is a plan to build a .20g EZB. What I am
saying is this. For those who understand bracing wire it seems like a great
fix. For the people at local contest who ask me why it matters which way
they orient the wood grain on their motor sticks, telling them that the
need to tie a bracing thread to three for four spots on their motor stick
to make their plane fly probably isn't going to help their confusion.

I would advocate for a LPP with a prohibition against composites if we're
looking to help beginners rather than adding another control mechanism to
the model.

I apologize if you or Bill too my comment as a personal attack. It was not
intended that way.
