I also use Bestine as it is pure heptane with no other hydrocarbons present (to the beter than 0.1% level). That said, it is expensive. John Kagan uses plain naphtha (or lighter fluid from his stoogie stash) which one can get for less at many hardware and paint stores. Naphtha is contains a mixture of various hydrocarbons of which heptane is usually a major fraction. Finally, Coleman Premium Blended Fuel is mostly heptane with some (up to 25% or so) hexane and a percent at most other stuff like xylene. Coleman fuel, however, is pinkish in color if that bothers you. I use Coleman fuel for general cleanup but not for any actual building. I buy it on sale after camping season ends.

Leo, Bloomington IN
