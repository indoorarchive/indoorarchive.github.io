At the risk of sounding like broken record, I'll put in my 2 cents as an indoor newbi.


I've been building FF models for over 40 years, but indoor only the last 2 years. My first model was a LPP built with light outdoor wood and covered in indoor mylar. It was relatively easy to build to weight and flies great for me. Since I had been using mylar on my outdoor rubber models, I found the transition to indoor fairly easy.


The second model I built was an A-6. It, too, flies great for me, but the construction process was much more difficult due to having to source indoor wood for the framework in order to make weight with the 450mg sq" paper I have. It still came out slightly overweight and the paper covering was harder to make look good.


I have since built 3 ministicks and an F1L. The ministicks are, by far, the most difficult to build due to the wood sizes involved.


If a beginner would ask me what to build, I would recommend the LPP then the F1L. As it is now, I wouldn't recommend the A-6. Allowing indoor mylar would make it much more desirable as a first model.


Rey
