Actually, my notes have 98' to the bottom of the disk and 76' to the bottom of the hanging mushroom. I get 99' at the portion of one edge of the disk but I count the height as 98' in all my plane notes. The plastic covered edge goes up 6' or more . I usually get 105' when I measure where the plane bounces but one of my notes have has 108' for a max height of the plastic shroud when I bounced many times in 2013. In 2013 and again this year, my F1L glanced the bottom of the highest girder but I was too excited worrying about my plane to measure anything!

 BTW, the WB site gets to be Cat III even with the extra headroom since 49' out from the disk, the girders are less than 30m high. This is similar to the environments at the Kent Cat II site and the Atlanta Cat I site.
