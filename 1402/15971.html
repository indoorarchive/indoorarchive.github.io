My old milligram scale starts to show the symptoms you described, with the
values and zero point drifting. So I decided it is time to get a new one.
Did not pay much attention to the range, until reading your note, and
realized that the new scale is actually up to 30mg! (similar to this one:
http://www.ebay.com/itm/Trendy-30g-0-001g-LCD-Digital-Portable-Jewelry-Pocket-Scale-G-GN-CT-OZ-OZT-DWT-/400517095447?pt=US_Pocket_Digital_Scales&hash=item5d40ade017)
A closer look revealed, that while the plastic chassis is similar, the
scale is in some parts different: the weighting platform is larger than in
the old type of scale. Also the electronics seem different, the new one has
very pronounced delay when turning it (the button needs to be pressed for
two seconds before it powers up - I was first fooled to think that I have a
defective unit as it did not power up - I though!). So maybe the new
electronics has more than 14 bit AD, too? It seems to give similar reading
and similar resolution as my old milligram scale. (Also the slot for test
weight not has a bubble level in it.)
