Nick,

  Several instances while timing flights I have had to start and stop a third watch extremely fast because plane being steered by balloon was
being hauled fair distance with propeller running then suddenly to stop, if for only a moment, repeatedly . Believe I did a good job but always looked at the stop watch after steering was completed to make sure watch wasn't still running from last click. I've not concluded all steering time should be deducted or stay the way it is today. From timers stand point only it can get intense starting and stopping steering clock. In circumstance explained above and yours of tapping the plane with pole how is a timer to know clock shouldn't be started because it is only going to be for a thousandths of second?

Mark Schaefer

  Totally impractical and impossible. You cannot deduct thousandths of

  F1D's - some may be unfamiliar with modelling of any sort. The prop
  stop time recorded by GBR timekeepers in Belgrade tallied with my own
  watch exactly and the prop stop time is the most important factor
  here. The timekeepers in Belgrade were very very well briefed and on
  the ball and no major errors occurred.

  I saw no evidence whatsoever of altitude gain through pointing a
  model into wind. Although the drift was bad, we weren't sailing model
  racing yachts in belgrade.

  as did all TM's. Again, it's the same for all so no advantage for
