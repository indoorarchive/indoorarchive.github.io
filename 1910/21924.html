Hi all,
I did some googling about the reasons for this shutdown. In short, Yahoo
groups are pre-social network information exchange tool.
So, giant IT company recognized it's out of date and shutting it down.
Why are we trying to save 18 years old IT technology? There were talks here
about saving the archive, 100Mb or something. These days it's nothing.

Personally, I find it of very little use for my indoor free flight
activities (does not mean it's a bad/useless thing).
My time goes ~ 75% to Facebook, ~20% to indoornewsandviews and the
remaining ~5% to HIP forum.

If we want to attract new people to the hobby, we would have to keep up
with modern IT.
By the way, my kids, (10yo and 15yo), say that Facebook is for old farts.
Cool kids hang out in Snap Chat and Instagram.


Regards,

> Which gets us to the heart of the matter: None of these offering are
> really ideal for sharing information. They compete with each other
> somewhat, each offering having their own strengths and weaknesses. In a
> sense, we need curated content that is easy to search. We need to be able
> to ask question, learn from each other and share our knowledge.

> 1. Basic emailed questions/threads?
> 2. Daily digests?
> 3. Web front end to read/search group threads? (must have good search
> tool!)
> 4. File sharing?
> 5. RSS feed?
> 6. What else?
