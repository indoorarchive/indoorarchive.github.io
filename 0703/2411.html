It sounds like you need more turns, and you need to improve your winding techniques I sometimes use the size of rubber you are using in Cat 1 sites and can get 1300 or more turns in it at .55 in oz. I would wind harder and back off to a launch torque your plane can handle. A couple hundred more turns will by you another 4 to 5 minutes without changing anything with your plane. Are you sure your pitch is 30" your RPM seems low with that low of pitch.
Are you sure your motor sticks cannot take more torque, if they can't for sure that should be your next building goal higher sites will need more launch torque.

The rubber testing program I use is also a good tool for improving your winding skills it will run in MS Excel and is not hard to use. It can be downloaded off my cloubuster web page or I can send it as an attachment to a off list email to you.
I would put my time into propellers, winding skills and stronger motor sticks.

Good luck
Fred
