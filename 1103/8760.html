The bottom rotor runs in the downwash of the top rotor, so benefits from higher pitch.
Some use very low pitch in top ~10deg, & 60-90deg in bottom.
 
As to stability, many light indoor heli's can't be launched straight enuf down to hit the floor before recovery.
Have never seen one launched horizontal that didn't make a quick turn for the vertical. This included a "reversible" in NFFS SYMPO88 which was stable when wound in either direction! Defies all logic & probably some physical laws.

Be happy, don't worry,
