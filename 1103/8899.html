To All,

My new A2Z winder worked pretty well. I'm glad I bought it!

I still haven't figured out how to "read" the counter at-a-glance. So I
still wound my motors basically by torque. I need to work with it a lot
more, and that's my fault, not the winder's!.

The only thing that isn't quite right, is the pointer needle is somewhat
loose, so I'll have to figure out a way to fix that.

As pricey as it was, I'm surprised that something this complex and high
in
quality can still be built in the US for this kind of money. Maybe
manufacturing in the US isn't extinct yet. The big machine shops just
need
to think like model builders!
