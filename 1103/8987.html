On 3/30/2011 3:25 PM, Benjamin Saks wrote:
> I remember back in my youth of chasing the Junior AMA Helicopter
> record that there were old designs which used this practice to kill
> the top rotor spin and get longer flight times. I could be wrong but I
> think that they made flights like this grounds for disqualification.

There was a large controversy over this at one USIC years back that I
attended where the stopped upper rotors were allowed as official flight,
flight times went up ~40-50% then people tried to jam the rotor since
the CD allowed it. When I was chasing the helicopter record for Cat 1
at a local gym I had a model that normally would do 6-7 minutes suddenly
do 16 minutes when the upper rotor got stuck then fell out of the tile.
A few flights later I got 22+ minutes and it ran out of turns and was
still stuck in the ceiling tile.
