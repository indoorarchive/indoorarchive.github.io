For my A6 record flight I took the model out of the box and began flying
with no trim changes from the last time it was flown in Atlanta. My
first flight was launched at .09 in-oz and didn't get high enough. the
next flight at .11 in-oz had multiple hangups. At .10 in-oz I had less
hangups. At .096 in-oz I had a couple of light touches and did 7:26 for
the record.

After making new flying surfaces in December the same model and prop has
set records in Cat 1, 2 and 4.

Various combinations of bad steering, hangups and broken motors ruined
my record attempts in F1M and LPP. My F1L actually flew - which it
refused to do in Tustin and Colorado - but couldn't keep up with the
junior members of the GPL group (Leo and Kang).
