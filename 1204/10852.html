The A6 I hoped to do well with at Kent was a low aspect ratio model with tip plates. Very similar to a slightly larger mini stick. The idea was to be able to fly in tight circles between the beams and take full advantage of the ceiling height. The prop was not a VP, but built to flair. The tight circles and low aspect ratio did not produce satisfactory times during Saturdays record trial. I switched to my standard high ratio model and did 7:10 just touching a light once. Bill later put up a 7:26 for the record. I was hoping to better that on Sunday during the contest with my standard A6. The model however seemed to have the Velcro syndrome, hanging up on each of the five official flight attempts.


Tom Sova
