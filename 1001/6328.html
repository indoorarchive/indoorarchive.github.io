Hi. I have been using 4 strand motors on my F1m and sometimes on F1d. The main benefit for me is that they do not break catastrophically in flight as the knots hold back the loose ends after a break.
My F1m can be launched at around 90g/cm torque, the stripped rubber is around 1.1g/m or slightly less. (march 02)
The strands need to be evenly lubricated and carefully stripped or they will have a shorter life as opposed to the more common two strand motor.
 
Regards, Gerard.

There's another accidental benefit involved. My high ceiling F1M motors - which I've made a lot of - are usually in the .075 to .080 range. The left over part of the strand can sometimes be used either for A6, F1L or a 3 strand F1M motor depending on the thickness of the off-cut.
