Hey guys,

We've got fantastic opportunity to fly Indoor at the EAA museum in Oshkosh (b'Gosh) WI, April 1-3 2011.

This site probably isn't for record setting, but the air is great and the extra benefits are unbeatable: the EAA AirVenture Museum!

Check out a live webcam of the Eagle Hanger here: http://www.airventuremuseum.org/webcam/ (hint: check out the "Antique/Classic" cam to see my 2000 World Champion F1D on display at the entrance to the "Little wings…Big dreams" display – right over the Wright Flyer)

Check out the virtual tour of Eagle Hanger here: http://www.airventuremuseum.org/virtual/warbirds1.asp (they move the planes in the center out to make room for us to fly)

The contest will be Friday and Saturday afternoon and evening, emphasizing HLG/CLG, LPP, EZB, F1L, and Ministick. All other Indoor events are welcome, too (I'll make award certificates for events with more than three entrants).

If there is enough interest, I could be persuaded to make this an F1D regional.

Saturday and Sunday days will be the EAA Family Flightfest, with a bunch of kids and parents tossing around Guillow's slide-together gliders. Show off with your favorite demo models and/or help the kids make their gliders fly.

Use the attached form to register (pre-registration required)

Let's make a good showing here. It's going be a lot of fun!

p.s. The onsite lodge might be available for us to stay in. More info to follow.
